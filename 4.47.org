* Question
Louis Reasoner suggests that, since a verb phrase is either a verb or a verb phrase followed by a prepositional phrase, it would be much more straightforward to define the procedure ~parse-verb-phrase~ as follows (and similarly for noun phrases):

#+begin_src scheme
(define (parse-verb-phrase)
  (amb (parse-word verbs)
       (list 'verb-phrase
             (parse-verb-phrase)
             (parse-prepositional-phrase))))
#+end_src

Does this work? Does the program's behavior change if we interchange the order of expressions in the ~amb~?

* Answer
Louis's approach will get stuck in a loop after exhausting all valid options.

~(parse-verb-phrase)~, which always succeeds, opens a nested ~amb~ that ~(parse-verb-phrase)~, which always fails, causes the evaluator to backtrack to.

#+begin_src scheme
  (amb ... ;; 3. Loop is complete
       (amb 'verb-phrase
            (amb <verb>
                 ...) ;; 2. Continues downward
            (parse-prepositional phrase))) ;; 1. Fails
#+end_src

The procedure in the text avoids this by computing ~(parse-verb-phrase)~ once and extending ~(parse-prepositional phrase)~ as needed.

#+begin_src scheme
(define (parse-verb-phrase)
  (define (maybe-extend verb-phrase)
    (amb verb-phrase
         (maybe-extend (list 'verb-phrase
                             verb-phrase
                             (parse-prepositional-phrase)))))
  (maybe-extend (parse-word verbs)))
#+end_src

Interchanging the expressions will cause an immediate recursion, while Louis's approach recurses only after all options are exhausted.
